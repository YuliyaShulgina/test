About the Artist

"I paint the moment before recognition — when something is sensed but not yet named."

Mara Solène (b. 1981, Lyon) is a Paris-based painter whose practice orbits the threshold between figuration and pure sensation. Trained at the École nationale supérieure des Beaux-Arts, her work has been acquired by private collections across Europe, Asia, and North America. She works in oil, mixed media, and large-format charcoal, often spending months on a single canvas before considering it resolved.

The Practice

How a painting begins

01
The Archive

Every work begins with weeks of drawings, photographs, and found materials — a private visual language built from observation and memory.

02
Ground & Tone

Canvas or linen is toned in earth pigments. The ground is not neutral — it carries the emotional temperature of everything painted over it.

03
Build & Erase

Layers accumulate and are destroyed. Erasure is as significant as mark-making. A painting reveals itself through what is removed.

04
The Resolution

A work is finished when it holds a specific quality of tension — not resolved, exactly, but still. A painting that is too comfortable is not yet done.
